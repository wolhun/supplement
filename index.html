Low choline levels lead to trouble concentrating — people who don’t get enough of it display symptoms of Attention Deficit Hyperactivity Disorder (ADHD). One study found that children with ADHD had a dramatically reduced amount of the acetylcholine receptor[*]. If you struggle with ADHD, alpha-GPC may help.

 

And if you don’t have ADHD and just want better brain function, alpha-GPC is still worth your time. Healthy people who took alpha-GPC showed better reaction time and increased the ability to focus on a mentally fatiguing task .

Alpha-GPC gives your brain the building blocks for acetylcholine, a neurotransmitter that plays a fundamental role in memory formation.

 

Study after study has found that alpha-GPC improves cognitive function in humans.

One study, for example, gave people alpha-GPC or placebo for ten days before prescribing them with an amnesia-inducing drug called scopolamine. The results showed that supplementation blocks memory impairments caused by scopolamine.

 

Other research has found that alpha-GPC helps recover age-related memory loss, including memory improvement in people with Alzheimer’s disease.

More recent studies suggest that alpha-GPC may benefit power output and athletic performance.

 

Healthy young men who took a single dose of alpha-GPC showed increased growth hormone secretion and fat-burning within an hour. At the 60-minute mark, their growth hormone had increased by 290%. The levels returned back to baseline after two hours.

 

Higher growth hormone means faster muscle recovery and increased fat oxidation (or fat burning). If you want to see faster results in the gym, consider taking alpha-GPC 30-60 minutes before working out.

Many people get confused between dementia and Alzheimers. So, it is essential to establish the difference between the two first. Dementia is a general term used to describe a decline in a person’s mental ability to an extent that the decline begins to interfere with the person’s day-to-day tasks. Alzheimer’s is not dementia but a specific disease that is one of the most common causes of dementia.

 

Over the years, several studies have been conducted to study the effect of alpha GPC intake on dementia patients. In conclusion, people suffering from dementia can benefit immensely from taking 1000 mg of Alpha GPC per day dosage.   1. Treat Transient Ischemic Attacks

 

Transient ischemic attacks (TIA) are brief episodes of neurological dysfunction, often caused by an interruption to the supply of blood to the brain and eye. Transient ischemic attacks usually last for only a few minutes but they are seen as precursors of stroke. A study was conducted to study the effect of Alpha GPC on stroke and TIA patients. The study revealed that people who took 1200-mg Alpha GPC shots every day for 28 days, followed by 1200 mg/day of Alpha GPC (through oral measures) for six months, showed better-thinking skills and as well as better recovery from TIA.
